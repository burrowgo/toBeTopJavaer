### CET
Central European Time (CET) is one of the names for the time zone that is one hour ahead of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). It is used by most European countries and some North African countries. During the winter, it is UTC+1, and during the summer (Daylight Saving Time), it is UTC+2.

### UTC
Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) is the primary time standard by which the world regulates clocks and time. The acronym comes from the English "Coordinated Universal Time" and French "Temps Universel Coordonne". In Mainland China, it is referred to as International Coordinated Time according to GB/T 7408. UTC is a time system based on atomic time and is kept as close as possible to Universal Time (UT).

### GMT
Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) refers to the standard time at the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London, through which the Prime Meridian passes.

### CST
China Standard Time (CST) is the standard time for China. It is in the UTC+8 time zone, 8 hours ahead of Coordinated Universal Time. It is the same as the National Standard Time in Taiwan, Hong Kong Time, and Macau Standard Time. When it is 0:00 AM GMT, it is 8:00 AM CST.

### Relationships

* CET = UTC/GMT + 1 hour
* CST = UTC/GMT + 8 hours
* CST = CET + 7 hours
