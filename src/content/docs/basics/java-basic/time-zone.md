---
title: "Time Zone"
---

A time zone is a region on Earth that uses the same definition of time. Previously, people determined time by observing the position of the sun (hour angle), which resulted in different times for places at different longitudes (local time). In 1863, the concept of a time zone was first used. Time zones partially solve this problem by establishing a standard time for a region.

Various countries in the world are located at different positions on the Earth, so sunrise and sunset times in different countries, especially countries with large east-west spans, must have some deviation. These deviations are the so-called time differences.

In order to cater to the convenience of use in various regions and to make it easy for people in other places to convert local time to the time of other places, relevant international conferences decided to divide the Earth's surface into regions from east to west according to meridians, and stipulate that the time difference between adjacent regions is 1 hour. People at the eastern and western ends of the same region see the sun rise at most 1 hour apart. When people cross a region, they adjust their clocks by 1 hour (subtract 1 hour westward, add 1 hour eastward), and add or subtract a few hours when crossing several regions. This is very convenient to use. Today, the world is divided into 24 time zones. Since in practice a country or a province often spans two or more time zones at the same time, for administrative convenience, a country or a province is often grouped together. Therefore, time zones are not strictly divided according to north-south straight lines, but according to natural conditions. For example, China has a vast territory and spans almost 5 time zones, but for simplicity and convenience, in practice only the standard time of the East Eight Time Zone, namely Beijing Time, is used.
