---
title: "Protobuf"
---

Protocol Buffer (Protobuf) is a high-performance, cross-language, and cross-platform serialization library from Google.

Created in early 2001, Protobuf was initially an internal Google project known as "proto1." It has evolved continuously within Google to meet developer needs, adding many new features and becoming a dependency for numerous internal libraries. Almost every Google developer uses it.

When Google began open-sourcing its internal projects, they decided to open-source Protobuf first due to these widespread dependencies.

The current stable version of Protobuf is 3.9.2, released on September 23, 2019. Because many companies adopted Protobuf early on, many projects still use the proto2 protocol. Consequently, both proto2 and proto3 are currently in widespread use.
