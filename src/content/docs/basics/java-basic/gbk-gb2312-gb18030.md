---
title: "Gbk Gb2312 Gb18030"
---

All three are encoding methods that support Chinese characters, with GBK being the most commonly used.

The following content is from CSDN and is quite detailed.

GB2312 (1980): A 16-bit character set, containing 6,763 simplified Chinese characters and 682 symbols, for a total of 7,445 characters.
Pros: Suitable for simplified Chinese environments, it is a Chinese national standard, used in mainland China, Singapore, and other places.
Cons: Not compatible with traditional Chinese; its collection of Chinese characters is too small.

GBK (1995): A 16-bit character set, containing 21,003 Chinese characters and 883 symbols, for a total of 21,886 characters.
Pros: Suitable for environments where simplified and traditional Chinese coexist, used by simplified Windows (code page cp936), fully downward compatible with GB2312, and upwardly supports the ISO-10646 international standard; all characters can be mapped one-to-one to Unicode 2.0.
Cons: Not an official standard, requires conversion with Big5; many search engines do not support GBK characters well.

GB18030 (2000): A 32-bit character set, containing 27,484 Chinese characters, along with major ethnic minority scripts such as Tibetan, Mongolian, and Uyghur.
Pros: Can include all characters and symbols you can think of, it is China's latest national standard.
Cons: Currently, fewer software applications support it.
