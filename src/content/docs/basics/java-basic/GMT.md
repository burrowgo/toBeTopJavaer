---
title: "Gmt"
---

Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) refers to the local mean solar time at the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, a suburb of London, England, because the prime meridian is defined as the longitude line passing through there.

Since February 5, 1924, the Greenwich Observatory has been responsible for broadcasting time adjustment information to the world every hour.

Noon in Greenwich Mean Time refers to the time when the mean sun crosses the Greenwich meridian (that is, when it is at its highest point in the sky above Greenwich). Since the earth's rotation is somewhat irregular every day and is slowly decelerating, GMT, based on the defects of astronomical observation itself, has been replaced by Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) reported by atomic clocks.

GMT+8 is generally used to represent the time in China because China is located in the East Eighth Zone, and the time is 8 hours faster than Greenwich Mean Time.
